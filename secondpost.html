Global Food Insecurity

Conflicts, climate change, and the COVID-19 pandemic are all responsible for a 10% increase in Food Insecurity. If this issue persists, more serious consequences will follow.

Posted by Kylee Henney on April 28, 2023

It's crucial to comprehend what food insecurity is before delving into how it now affects the world. Food insecurity is described as the inability of all members of a home to consistently have access to enough food for an active, and healthy lifestyle. The entire world's population is growing more worried about the future as a result of new wars and escalating tensions developing in third-world nations with significant food industries.

The Affects of the Russia-Ukraine War

The Ukraine's war with Russia prevents the export of wheat, grain, and maize. Food supplies in Ukraine are constrained as a result of Russia attacking multiple grain silos. Russia has also stopped all exports and imports to Ukraine. Consequently, there are minimal resources available to support Ukraine's war efforts. Every year, Ukraine exported two thirds of its grain, but due to recent events, no grain is being exported. The cost of breakfast items including cereal, bread, and oats has increased as a result of this conflict.

"Russia's invasion of Ukraine has provoked massive hikes in food prices and risk of food shortages. We need to act now and the way we address global food insecurity will be decisive for Europe's geopolitical standing" -Josep Borrell

Most cilivans think that food prices have gone up because of the war. There are tensions not only between nations but also between people. instead of joining together, they opted to support their government. Everyone is the enemy. Families worry about being able to buy food at high prices, which has contributed to an increase in stress levels worldwide. Civilians desire both international peace and a plentiful food supply.

The Affects of COVID-19 Pandemic

The world has been negatively impacted by COVID-19. It did, however, deplete the food supply, causing food shortages and, in certain places, like Africa, famine.
